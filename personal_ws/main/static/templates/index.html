About

Born in New York City, moved to Montclair, NJ. Attended Johns Hopkins University in Baltimore, MD (2008-2012). Rode my bicycle from Baltimore to Berkeley (part of the 4K for Cancer). Began my time at UC Berkeley in the astronomy department, funded through The Berkeley Fellowship and the National Science Foundation Graduate Research Fellowship.

Research

I work with Eliot Quataert on the thermodynamic history of gas accreted on to galaxies and groups. Previously, with Chris McKee and Aristotle Socrates, I investigated the impact of turbulence in molecular clouds on the orientation of protoplanetary disks relative to the spin axis of their host stars. As an undergraduate I did two small research projects, one with Ned Wright at UCLA on the thermodynamics of cratered asteroids for comparison with WISE observations, and one with Michal Simon at Stony Brook on using the equivalent width of Na lines as an age indicator for nearby young stars.
